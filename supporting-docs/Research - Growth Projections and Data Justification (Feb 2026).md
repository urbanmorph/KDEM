## 2.3 Key Headwinds Justifying Lower Projections

### A. AI Disruption of IT Services Revenue
- Bessemer Venture Partners (Oct 2025): AI could reduce IT services contract values by **8-10% over 3-4 years**
- India IT headcount could decline from 7.5-8M to 6M by 2031 (base case)
- NASSCOM projects only **6-7% IT growth in FY27** in the AI era
- AI decouples revenue from headcount, fundamentally changing billing models

### B. Global IT Spending Moderation
- Gartner: 10.8% global IT spending growth in 2026, but skewed by AI infrastructure
- Traditional IT services spending (where Karnataka earns most revenue) grows at a more modest pace

### C. ESDM Semiconductor Timeline Risk
- Tata Electronics Gujarat fab: production expected late 2026
- Karnataka's ISMC fab (Tower Semiconductor): no confirmed production timeline, multiple delays
- Full-scale multi-facility operations: 2027-2028 at earliest
- Revenue ramp from new fabs typically takes 2-3 years after production start

### D. Startup Funding Winter
- India startup funding dropped 17% in 2025 to $10.5B
- Seed-stage funding fell 30% in 2025
- Number of funding rounds fell 39% YoY
- Structural shift toward revenue visibility and unit economics

### E. Currency Depreciation
- If rupee depreciates 2-3% annually vs USD, cumulative 10-15% compression in USD terms over 5-7 years

### F. Digitizing Sectors Data Quality
- Based on McKinsey 2019 "potential economic value" estimates (aspirational, not forecasts)
- Sub-sector CAGRs of 20-227% are implausible
- Dashboard rates this at 2/5 confidence

# Part 3: Justification of the $168 Billion Current Digital Economy Estimate

## 3.1 Executive Summary

The KDEM Dashboard reports Karnataka's current digital economy size as approximately **$168 billion (FY 2024-25)**. This figure is derived by summing five core verticals:

| Vertical | FY 2024-25 Value | Share of Total | Confidence | Primary Source |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|------------|----------------|
| IT Exports | $85.12B | 50.6% | 4/5 | NASSCOM Strategic Review FY25 |
| ESDM | $36.69B | 21.8% | 5/5 | MeitY + Care Edge Research |
| Newly Digitizing | $20.23B | 12.0% | 2/5 | KDEM Excel (McKinsey framework) |
| IT Domestic | $17.46B | 10.4% | 4/5 | NASSCOM Strategic Review FY25 |
| Startups | $8.79B | 5.2% | 3/5 | KDEM Excel CAGR projection |
| **TOTAL** | **$168.29B** | **100%** | **~3.5 avg** | |

83% of the total ($139B) comes from high-confidence NASSCOM/MeitY sources. The remaining 17% ($29B) relies on KDEM Excel projections.

The formula is documented in `src/services/referenceData.js`, line 413:
```
currentTotalDigital_USD_Bn: 168   // 85.12 + 17.46 + 36.69 + 8.79 + 20.23 = 168.29
```

---

## 3.2 Vertical 1: IT Exports -- $85.12 Billion

### Methodology
- **India Total IT Exports FY25:** $224 billion (NASSCOM Strategic Review 2025)
- **Karnataka Share Assumed:** 38%
- **Calculation:** $224B x 38% = $85.12B

### Source Verification

**NASSCOM Strategic Review 2025** confirms India's IT export revenue at $224 billion for FY2024-25, with total IT industry revenue (including hardware) at $282.6 billion. Exports grew 4.6% YoY. Total industry talent: 5.8 million.

**Karnataka's share -- the critical question:** The dashboard uses 38%, but independent verification shows Karnataka's actual share is likely higher:

| Source | Karnataka Share | Context |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| STPI FY2024-25 | 44% | Of India's total IT and electronic hardware exports |
| PIB (Shri Arvind Kumar, 2025) | 42% | Karnataka's IT export contribution |
| STPI FY2022-23 | 41.86% | Of India's total STPI exports |
| KDEM Dashboard assumption | 38% | Conservative estimate |

**Important distinction:** STPI figures track STPI-registered units (a subset of total IT exports). The NASSCOM $224B covers all IT exports. The 38% is a "total IT exports" share, lower than the STPI-measured share because non-STPI exports from other states dilute Karnataka's percentage.

### Confidence: 4/5 stars
- India-level NASSCOM number: very reliable (5/5)
- The 38% KA share: reasonable, possibly conservative given STPI data

### Potential Bias
- **Understatement risk:** At 42%, IT Exports would be $94.1B (+$9B)
- **Overstatement risk:** Low. The 38% is conservative.

### Methodology
- **India Total IT Domestic FY25:** $58.2 billion (NASSCOM Strategic Review 2025)
- **Karnataka Share Assumed:** 30%
- **Calculation:** $58.2B x 30% = $17.46B

### Confidence: 4/5 stars

### Potential Bias

- **Understatement risk:** Low.

## 3.4 Vertical 3: ESDM -- $36.69 Billion

### Methodology
- **Source:** MeitY + Care Edge Research, FY2024-25 actual
- **Karnataka Share Assumed:** 20% of India ESDM
- The code shows `indiaFY25: null`, suggesting the $36.69B may be a direct Karnataka figure

### Source Verification

**India's total electronics production FY2024-25:** Rs 11.3 lakh crore (~$134.5B at Rs 84/$).

If Karnataka's ESDM share is 20%: $134.5B x 20% = $26.9B -- significantly less than $36.69B.

However, KDEM's website states Karnataka contributes:
- **10%** of the country's electronics output (production)
- **40%** of the national share in electronic design
- **50%** in electronic product companies

The 20% appears to be a blended figure covering both production and design. The $36.69B implies Karnataka represents ~27% of India's ESDM.

**Cross-check against base year:** FY2021-22 base was $17.98B, FY2024-25 is $36.69B. Implied CAGR: ~27%. India's total grew from Rs 6.4L Cr to Rs 11.3L Cr (CAGR ~21%). Karnataka growing faster is plausible given aggressive ESDM investment.

### Confidence: 5/5 stars (as rated in codebase)
- Based on MeitY actual data
- However, discrepancy between 10% production share and effective 27% warrants caution

### Potential Bias
- **Overstatement risk:** Significant if KDEM includes electronic design services (where KA dominates) beyond pure manufacturing
- **Definitional ambiguity:** "ESDM" may mean different things to different sources

## 3.7 Where the Estimate Could Be Wrong

### Potential Overstatement

| Area | Potential Impact |
|------|-----------------|
| Digitizing sectors CAGRs of 20-227% | Could reduce $20.2B by 30-50% (-$6-10B) |
| ESDM at effective 27% share vs 10% production share | Could reduce $36.7B by up to 25% (-$10B) |
| Startup 30% CAGR too aggressive | Could reduce $8.8B by 20-30% (-$2-3B) |
| Double-counting (startups in IT exports) | Unknown, possibly -$2-5B |
| **Total potential overstatement** | **-$10-25B** |

### Potential Understatement

| Area | Potential Impact |
|------|-----------------|
| IT Exports at 38% when STPI shows 42-44% | Could add +$9-13B |
| GCC revenue not fully captured in IT Exports | Could add +$5-10B |
| **Total potential understatement** | **+$9-15B** |

### Net Assessment

The $168B is likely within a reasonable **$150-180B range**, but the composition differs from what's shown. IT Exports is likely understated while ESDM and Digitizing Sectors are likely overstated -- they roughly cancel out.

## 3.8 Recommendations for Improving Data Quality

1. **Obtain NASSCOM state-wise breakdown.** KDEM should formally request Karnataka-specific data to replace the assumed 38% and 30% shares.

2. **Validate ESDM figure with MeitY.** Clarify whether $36.69B is (a) MeitY's official KA figure, (b) derived from share assumption, or (c) includes electronic design services.

3. **Replace Startup CAGR projection with actuals.** Use Tracxn, Startup Genome, or DPIIT data for actual Karnataka startup revenue.

4. **Retire McKinsey 2019 framework for Digitizing Sectors.** Replace with actual data: GST digital transactions, NPCI/UPI volumes, GeM procurement data, or India's Digital Economy Satellite Account (when available from MoSPI).

5. **Conduct double-counting audit.** A SaaS startup exporting from Bengaluru appears in both "Startups" and "IT Exports." Establish clear definitional boundaries.

6. **Add error bands.** Display $150-180B range with confidence interval instead of a point estimate.

7. **Establish annual data reconciliation.** Partner with Karnataka's Directorate of Economics and Statistics for an official repeatable methodology.
