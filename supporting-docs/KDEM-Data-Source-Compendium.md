## Section 2: B3 — ESDM Beyond Bengaluru Apportionment

**The Question:** DB has 60% Beyond Bengaluru (BB) for ESDM; Excel has 5% BB. Which is correct?

### Key Findings

**Current ESDM landscape in Karnataka is overwhelmingly Bengaluru-centric:**

1. **BTS 2025 ESDM Directory:** 704 companies in Karnataka — 513 manufacturing, 61 design, 35 GCCs, 95 others. Virtually all headquartered in Bengaluru.

2. **Design segment (60% of ESDM value):** Karnataka has ~40% of India's electronic design output. Nearly 100% of this is in Bengaluru — design requires talent clusters that only Bengaluru provides.

3. **Foxconn Devanahalli:** $2.8B investment, 30,000 employees, manufacturing iPhone 17 and components. Located in Bengaluru North (Devanahalli near airport). This counts as "Bengaluru" geography, not Beyond Bengaluru.

4. **Mysuru EMC (Electronics Manufacturing Cluster):** 7 Letters of Intent (LOIs) only; Rs 1,300 Cr proposed investment; zero production as of early 2026. The ISMC $3B semiconductor fab originally planned for Mysuru has stalled. Silectric Rs 3,426 Cr display fab approved but in pre-construction phase.

5. **Tumakuru:** Land allocated for industrial development, but no anchor ESDM tenant. Foxconn chose Devanahalli instead.

6. **Dharwad EMC:** Rs 180 Cr approved under India EMC 2.0 scheme; 9 companies committed Rs 340 Cr; infrastructure is being built but not yet operational.

7. **KWIN City (Kempegowda World Industrial City):** 5,800 acres, Rs 40,000 Cr investment target — but located in Bengaluru Rural district, which is geographically "Bengaluru" not "Beyond Bengaluru."

### Analysis

The 60% BB figure in the DB appears to conflate cross-sector GIM investment distribution with ESDM-specific production geography. At the GIM 2025, total investment MoUs were distributed across Karnataka — but ESDM production specifically remains concentrated in Bengaluru.

**Why ESDM can't easily distribute beyond Bengaluru:**
- Electronic design (60% of sector value) requires deep talent pools — only available in Bengaluru
- Manufacturing needs supply chain proximity — currently clustered around Bengaluru
- Tier 2 EMCs (Mysuru, Dharwad) are in infrastructure build phase, not production
- Even "new" investments like Foxconn chose Bengaluru periphery (Devanahalli), not Tier 2

### Recommendation

**Current reality: 3-5% BB.** Realistic 2030 target: **15-25% BB** (driven by Dharwad EMC operationalization, Mysuru Silectric coming online, and manufacturing-only distribution).

**Suggested DB approach:** Year-tagged apportionment rules:
- FY25: 95% Bengaluru / 5% BB
- FY28: 85% Bengaluru / 15% BB
- FY32: 75% Bengaluru / 25% BB (optimistic, assumes EMCs deliver)

## Section 3: B5 — Overall Bengaluru/BB Revenue Split

**The Question:** KDEM policy says 15% Beyond Bengaluru; Excel model gives 3.7% BB. What's the real target?

### Key Findings

1. **STPI-Bengaluru jurisdiction FY25:** Rs 4,53,593 Cr (~$54B). This includes sub-centres at Mysuru, Mangaluru, Hubballi, Manipal, and Davanagere — but Bengaluru accounts for 95-98% of the total.

2. **KDEM Beyond Bengaluru program:** 126 companies operational, 5,500+ jobs created. This is significant as a program achievement but tiny relative to Bengaluru's IT ecosystem.

3. **KDEM's own published BB target:** ">5% of overall tech economy by 2030" (Daijiworld, quoting KDEM officials). This is notably less ambitious than the 15% policy figure.

4. **GCC Policy 2024-29:** 500 new GCCs + 3.5 lakh jobs by 2029. Includes Tier-2 rental reimbursement up to Rs 2 Cr/year to incentivize BB expansion.

5. **Mysuru IT exports FY23-24:** Rs 5,700 Cr (~$685M). The largest BB cluster by revenue.

6. **Mangaluru:** 150+ IT companies, 6,000+ jobs, growing at 15-18% annually. Vertex IT Park (1.5L sq ft) operational.

7. **Hubballi-Dharwad:** STPI center operational (37 startups incubated), 8 new KDEM-enabled companies. Revenue data not published.

## Section 4: B6 — Bengaluru Revenue vs GSDP Plausibility

**The Question:** Dashboard shows $337B for Bengaluru digital economy. Karnataka GSDP is ~$345B. Can one city's single sector approach state GDP?

### Key Findings

1. **Karnataka GSDP FY25:** Rs 28.09-28.83 lakh Cr (~$335-345B nominal at Rs 83-84/$).

2. **Karnataka GSDP FY26 (revised estimate):** Rs 30.70 lakh Cr (~$359B).

3. **Bengaluru Urban GDDP (all sectors combined):** Rs 8,59,154 Cr (~$102B). Source: Karnataka Economic Survey FY24. This is the total economic output of Bengaluru across ALL sectors — agriculture, manufacturing, services, everything.

4. **Bengaluru Metro GDP (PPP):** $359.9B. This is a Purchasing Power Parity figure, NOT nominal. PPP figures are typically 3-4x higher than nominal for Indian cities.

5. **Bengaluru Urban = 33.3% of Karnataka GSDP** (Karnataka Economic Survey FY24).

6. **Services sector = 63.2% of Karnataka GSDP** (NITI Aayog fiscal landscape 2025).

7. **NASSCOM "pass-through" issue:** Companies headquartered in Bengaluru bill through their Bengaluru registration but may deliver services from other cities. STPI registration is HQ-based, inflating Bengaluru's apparent output.

### Analysis

**The $337B Bengaluru digital economy figure is NOT plausible as nominal GDP.**

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Bengaluru total GDDP (all sectors) | ~$102B nominal | Karnataka Economic Survey |
| Bengaluru services sector (63.2% of GDDP) | ~$64B | Derived |
| Bengaluru IT/digital economy (realistic) | ~$54-70B | STPI data + estimates |
| KDEM Dashboard: Bengaluru digital economy | $337B | Implausible |
| Bengaluru Metro GDP (PPP) | $360B | PPP, not nominal |

**The confusion likely stems from:**
1. Conflating PPP Metro GDP ($360B) with nominal sector GDP
2. "Pass-through" billing — companies registered in Bengaluru may bill $54B through STPI, but the actual economic activity is distributed
3. The $350B KDEM target is for all of Karnataka's digital economy, not just Bengaluru

### Recommendation

**Clarify in dashboard communications:**
- The $329-351B target is Karnataka's total digital economy revenue across all verticals and geographies
- It is NOT Bengaluru's city-level GDP
- IT sector revenue ≠ GDP contribution (revenue includes input costs, intermediate goods, pass-through billing)
- Bengaluru's IT sector at nominal rates generates ~$54-70B in economic output

**If BB split increases to 15%:** Bengaluru share = $298B. This is still higher than Bengaluru's total GDDP ($102B), confirming the figure represents sector revenue (billing), not GDP contribution.

## Section 5: B7 — Karnataka Share of India IT

**The Question:** Dashboard uses 38% KA share. Is this accurate?

### Key Findings

Multiple methodologies yield different figures:

| Methodology | KA Share | Basis | Source |
|-------------|----------|-------|--------|
| STPI-only exports | 42-45% | KA STPI exports / India STPI exports | PIB, DG STPI Nov 2025 |
| STPI+SEZ combined | ~34% | KA total / India total (FY22) | Swarajya/MeitY |
| vs NASSCOM total ($224B) | 24% | KA STPI $54B / NASSCOM $224B | Derived from STPI + NASSCOM |
| Karnataka services exports (broader) | — | Rs 14,03,811 Cr ($159B FY25) | India Seatrade News |

**The discrepancy explained:**
- **STPI tracks by registration location.** A company registered at STPI-Bengaluru may deliver work from Pune, Hyderabad, or globally. The "pass-through" effect inflates Bengaluru/Karnataka figures.
- **NASSCOM includes non-STPI revenue.** The $224B includes BPM, ER&D, product revenue, and companies not registered under STPI.
- **The 38% figure** is a commonly cited estimate that falls between the STPI-only (42-45%) and STPI+SEZ (34%) figures. It is reasonable as a working estimate but includes pass-through inflation.
