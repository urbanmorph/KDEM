Karnataka's Advantages

  • Ecosystem Maturity: 45+ unicorns, 4,000+ investors vs nascent ecosystems elsewhere

Known Data Gaps & Mitigation Strategies

Gap: Company Registry Accuracy

High Priority

Problem: 126 companies in "Beyond Bengaluru" clusters claimed but only 26 verified with actual operations

Solution in Progress:

  • Company Registry System (Q1 2026) with annual verification
  • EPFO cross-reference for employment validation
  • Physical audit of cluster companies

Timeline: Full validation by Q3 2026

Gap: Tier-2/3 Export Attribution

Medium Priority

Problem: Service exports often routed through Bengaluru headquarters, making city-level attribution difficult

Solution in Progress:

  • STPI Nodal Centers to track location-specific exports
  • SEZ-specific export data (already available for Mysuru, Hubballi)
  • Request voluntary disclosure from major exporters

Timeline: Improved data by Q2 2026

Gap: Startup Ecosystem Health

Medium Priority

Problem: Limited longitudinal data on startup outcomes (survival, funding, exits)

Solution in Progress:

  • Karnataka Startup Cell annual cohort study (starting 2026)
  • Partnership with Tracxn for comprehensive tracking
  • Mandatory quarterly updates for incentive recipients

Timeline: Baseline established by Q4 2026

Gap: Global Benchmarking Data

Low Priority

Problem: Inconsistent metrics across global cities; some data proprietary/unavailable

Solution in Progress:

  • Partnership with Oxford Economics for standardized city data
  • Join global city networks (WCCD, ISO 37120 certified cities)
  • Peer city data exchange agreements (e.g., with Singapore IMDA)

Timeline: Ongoing improvement

Three-Tier Attribution Model

To handle causality challenges, we use a tiered framework for crediting KDEM impact:

Level A: Direct Attribution

Criteria: Companies/jobs/investments directly resulting from KDEM programs

Examples:

  • Startups graduated from KDEM incubators
  • Companies relocating due to KDEM incentives
  • Jobs in KDEM-funded infrastructure (innovation hubs, data centers)

Verification: Program records, signed agreements, incentive disbursements

Confidence: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

Level B: Influenced Attribution

Criteria: Growth enabled by KDEM ecosystem improvements, but not direct program participants

Examples:

  • Startups founded by talent trained in KDEM upskilling programs
  • Companies expanding due to improved infrastructure (KDEM-funded power, connectivity)
  • Investments attracted to regions with KDEM presence

Verification: Surveys, founder interviews, investor feedback

Confidence: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

Level C: Ecosystem Attribution

Criteria: Overall Karnataka digital economy growth during KDEM period

Examples:

  • Organic startup growth in Bengaluru (would have happened anyway)
  • National/global trends benefiting Karnataka
  • Pre-existing momentum from earlier policies (Beyond Bengaluru 2020)

Verification: Macro-level statistics, trend analysis, control region comparisons

Confidence: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (correlation, not causation)

Reporting Approach: KDEM reports focus on Level A (Direct) and Level B (Influenced) metrics. Level C (Ecosystem) is reported separately as contextual data. This ensures honest impact assessment without overclaiming credit.
